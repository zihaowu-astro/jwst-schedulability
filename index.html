JWST's sunshield restricts the boresight to a solar elongation of 85°–135°, so at any instant the observable sky is an annulus centered on the Sun. The nominal (Normal Roll) V3 position angle keeps the Sun in the V1–V3 plane; the allowed roll widens from ±3° near 85° to ±7° near 135°. Constraints follow the STScI JWST documentation.

Sun position uses the low-precision USNO formula (~0.01°). Developed by Zihao Wu (Harvard) with assistance from Claude Code.
